Social Statistics

A website about the effects of social media and healthier digital habits.

Social platforms shape how people communicate, learn, and spend time online. This site explores those effects and suggests better ways to manage them.

Updated on April 15, 2026 for a first deployment presentation.

Why it matters

Social media can be useful for connection and information, but heavy use can also affect attention, mood, and daily routines. Understanding both sides helps people make more intentional choices online.

Connection

People use social apps to stay in touch, share updates, and join communities around shared interests.

Attention

Frequent notifications and endless feeds can make it harder to focus on school, work, or rest.

Wellbeing

Using apps with purpose, rather than habit, can reduce stress and improve balance throughout the day.

Healthier habits

Small changes can make social media more helpful and less overwhelming. The goal is not to avoid it completely, but to use it with control and clear boundaries.

Manage notifications

Turn off alerts that are not important so the phone does not interrupt your attention all day.

Follow better accounts

Choose creators and pages that teach, inspire, or support your goals instead of filling your feed with noise.

Set time limits

Use screen-time limits or a schedule so app use stays under control and does not take over the day.
